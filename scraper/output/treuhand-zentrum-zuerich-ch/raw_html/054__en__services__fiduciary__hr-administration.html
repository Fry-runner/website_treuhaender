Frequently Asked Questions

What does payroll accounting include?

Payroll accounting includes all activities related to employee compensation. This includes:
  • Calculation and payment of wages
  • Processing of social insurance (AHV, IV, ALV, BVG, UVG)
  • Withholding tax returns
  • Preparation of payroll statements and wage certificates
  • Management of vacation, overtime, and absences
  • Year-end processing and notifications to authorities
Correct payroll accounting is crucial for compliance with legal requirements and employee trust. Treuhandzentrum ZÃ¼rich handles these tasks efficiently and reliably.

What is the difference between bookkeeping and payroll accounting?

Bookkeeping reflects the overall financial situation of a company. It includes the recording of business transactions such as income, expenses, investments, and the preparation of balance sheets and income statements.
 
Payroll accounting is a specialized area of bookkeeping. It relates exclusively to managing wages and processing all payroll-related social contributions and returns.
 
In short: Bookkeeping manages finances, payroll accounting manages personnel â both belong to the core business of a trustee.

What is payrolling?

Payrolling means the complete outsourcing of payroll administration to an external service provider such as Treuhandzentrum ZÃ¼rich. The trustee takes over the entire payroll processing including payroll statements, social insurance returns, withholding tax, and wage certificates.
 
For companies, payrolling offers an efficient solution to reduce administrative effort and ensure compliance with all legal requirements in labor law.

What is an expense regulation?

An expense regulation is a written guideline that determines which expenses of employees in connection with their professional activity are reimbursed â e.g., for meals, travel, accommodations, or representation costs.
 
A clearly formulated expense regulation creates transparency, prevents misunderstandings, and simplifies returns to the tax administration.

When must an expense regulation be approved?

An expense regulation must be approved by the responsible cantonal tax authority if:
  • Flat-rate expenses are to be paid out
  • the company wants to ensure that there are no taxable wage components
  • with multiple business locations or more complex structures
An approved regulation offers legal security and is particularly recognized as a binding basis in a tax audit.

Which employment contracts must be in writing?

In Switzerland, contract freedom applies in principle â meaning an employment contract can also be concluded orally. However, a written employment contract is required in the following cases:
  • Apprenticeship contracts (mandatory under the Vocational Training Act)
  • Fixed-term employment contracts with non-compete clauses or probation period extensions
  • Regulations on overtime, vacation, wages, or termination modalities that deviate from the law
  • Part-time contracts with employees subject to withholding tax (for documentation purposes)
  • A written contract protects both parties and creates clarity.

What are the legal notice periods in employment law?

The legal notice periods according to Art. 335c OR are as follows:
  • During probation period: 7 days on any date
  • In the 1st year of service: 1 month at month-end
  • From the 2nd to the 9th year of service: 2 months at month-end
  • From the 10th year of service: 3 months at month-end
Deviating regulations in the employment contract or collective agreement are permissible if they are recorded in writing. Compliance with notice periods is central to legal security in the employment relationship.
