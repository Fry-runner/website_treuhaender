Advance Directive

With an advance directive, you determine who is allowed to act on your behalf in case of incapacity â whether due to illness, accident, or age-related limitations. We help you correctly write your advance directive in Switzerland, formally validly deposit it, and tailor it to your personal situation. Whether you want to adapt an existing advance directive template as PDF or create a new directive individually â we accompany you professionally and reliably.

How long can you continue working after retirement?

After retirement, you can basically continue working indefinitely. There is no legal age limit for employment. Those who continue working after normal retirement age can waive part of the AHV deductions (annual allowance) and under certain circumstances even claim a deferred or postponed pension.

What types of prenuptial agreements are there?

In Switzerland, there are three possible marriage models that can be regulated by a prenuptial agreement:
  • Participation in acquired property (legal marital property regime, without prenuptial agreement)
  • Community of property (with prenuptial agreement)
  • Separation of property (with prenuptial agreement)

In Switzerland, a will can also be created without a notary â this is called a handwritten will. To be valid, it must:

A valid will in Switzerland must:
